Cross-Stressing Through Exercise

    “Before engaging in a new or different exercise program, first see your doctor.”



  • Exercise is a form of stress. Below are the shared features, enough of them that you can actually use exercise as a means by which to train yourself to better cope with stress. They are:
    1. Increased heart and respiration rate
    1. A switch from anabolism to catabolism
    1. Decreased salivationv
    1. Inhibition of digestion
  • How does this stress training program work? While each stressful experience and your response are unique, there are some features that occur in everyone. Thus, when your body learns to adapt to one form of stress, you are able to better cope with most other forms.
  • Calculate Your Maximum Heart Rate: Begin by calculating your maximum heart rate, which is the number of beats per minute (BPM) that cannot be exceeded when you increase the intensity of your exercise.

    Women: 226 – (age) = BPM

    Men: 220 – (age) = BPM

    Note that this calculation is not accurate but provides a point form which to start.
  • Determine Your Stress Zones: Next, calculate your high, medium, and low stress zones.
    1. Low Stress Zone is where you should start if you have not exercised before, or if you are recovering from an injury.

      Low Stress Zone

      Max HR _________ x 0.60 = ________ bpm

      Max HR _________ x 0.70 = ________ bpm


    1. Moderate Stress Zone. Your heart will beat at a faster rate because it will be bathed in larger amounts of adrenaline and other stress-related chemicals that speed your heart.

      Moderate Stress Zone

      Max HR _________ x 0.70 = ________ bpm

      Max HR _________ x 0.80 = ________ bpm


    1. High Stress Zone is the zone within which your body will learn to recover from an even greater amount of stress. When in this zone, your heart rate will be between 80 and 90 percent of your maximum. Your body will switch to anaerobic metabolism.

      High Stress Zone



      Max HR _________ x 0.80 = ________ bpm

      Max HR _________ x 0.90 = ________ bpm


  • Exercise is a form of stress, but it is one over which you have total control. You determine the start time, the intensity, as well as when to end the regimen.
  • Set Your Limits: Identify your personal stress zones using the formulas discussed above. The table below summarizes the heart rate that corresponds to the different zones.

    Low Stress 60 to 70 % of maximum heart rate
    Medium Stress 70 to 80 % of maximum heart rate
    High Stress 80 to 90 % of maximum heart rate



  • Plan on spending approximately 80 percent of your workout within the moderate stress zone. The remaining time should be spent oscillating between the low and high stress zones as you create a wave-like pattern of heart rate change.
  • Choosing A Form Of Exercise: It really doesn’t matter what form of exercise you choose. What is important is that you do something you enjoy. However, it is important to recognize that some exercise regimens enable you to attain a higher heart rate than others do.
